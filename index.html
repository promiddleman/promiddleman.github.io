Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our service

How does escrow protect me?

Escrow protection works by holding funds in a secure account managed by a trusted third party (us) until all conditions of the transaction are met. This protects both the buyer and seller from fraud, ensuring that the buyer receives what they paid for and the seller receives their payment.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept a wide range of payment methods including credit/debit cards, bank transfers, PayPal, and major cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum, and more. Our flexible payment options allow you to choose what works best for you.

How long does the escrow process take?

The time frame varies depending on your selected plan and the nature of the transaction. Basic plans process within 48 hours, Standard within 24 hours, and Premium within 12 hours after all verification requirements are met. The total transaction time also depends on how quickly both parties fulfill their obligations.

What happens if there's a dispute?

If a dispute arises, our team steps in to mediate between the parties. We'll review all transaction details, communications, and evidence submitted by both sides to reach a fair resolution. Premium plan members receive free dispute resolution services, while other plans may incur a small fee for this service.

Is my personal and financial information secure?

Absolutely. We employ bank-level encryption and security measures to protect all your data. Our platform is regularly audited by independent security firms, and we never store complete payment information on our servers. We comply with all relevant data protection regulations worldwide.

Can I use your service for international transactions?

Yes, ProMiddleman handles international transactions between parties in different countries. We support multiple currencies and have experience managing escrow for global commerce. Be aware that international transactions may require additional verification steps to comply with regulatory requirements.
